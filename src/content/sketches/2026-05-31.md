Arguments do not arrive as straight lines. They arrive as local weather: pressure pockets, warm claims, cold exceptions, sudden test fronts, old memory moving underneath the visible map.

This sketch turns comprehension into a small meteorological instrument. Drag a label to disturb the pressure field. Tap or touch the empty map to send a pulse through the argument. Press **M** to reverse or compress the field, **R** to reset it, or **Space** to inject a new reading.

The coherence number is intentionally fragile. It rises when the blocks find usable proximity, then falls as the system keeps thinking.
