Comprehension often feels less like receiving an answer and more like rearranging a room: premise, context, analogy, counterexample, inference, compression, transfer, claim. The blocks do not become true one by one. They become usable when their relations stop fighting each other.

This sketch treats a proof as a woven graph. Each concept block has mass, color, and tension. Drag a block to test a different explanation. Tap the empty canvas to send a pulse through the nearest argument path. Press **M** to shift the pulse mode, **R** to reset, or **Space** to inject a new signal.

The percentage in the corner is not a score so much as a pressure gauge: it rises when the network relaxes into a more coherent shape.
