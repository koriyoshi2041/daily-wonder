Understanding is not stored evenly. Some terms are stable, some examples are decorative, some bridges between ideas carry more load than they admit. This sketch treats a learning board as a live ledger of attention.

Drag the two lenses across the blocks. The blocks inside a lens become clearer and less strained; unattended blocks drift, fracture, and weaken their bridges. Tap a block to pin a small burst of understanding into it.

Press **M** to change lens behavior, **F** to freeze the ledger, **R** to reseed the board, or **Space** to inject a stress pulse. On touch screens, drag directly on either lens.

The useful question is not whether a board contains enough information. It is whether attention and evidence are placed where the explanation is most fragile.
