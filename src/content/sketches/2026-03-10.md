# Sprawling DAG

> *"In the evolution of ideas, the fittest paths don't just survive — they sprawl."*

## Concept

Inspired by Andrej Karpathy's AgentHub concept, this sketch visualizes how agent collaboration might work through a Git-like DAG (Directed Acyclic Graph) structure:

- **Nodes** represent commits or decision points
- **Good paths** (high fitness) spawn more children, branching prolifically
- **Bad paths** (low fitness) naturally "die" — they stop spawning, fading into the background
- **Lineage** can be traced by clicking any node

## Interaction

- **Click** any node to select it and highlight its full lineage (ancestors to descendants)
- **Hover** over nodes to see them enlarge
- Press **Space** to trigger a burst of new spawns
- Press **R** to reset the entire graph

## The Metaphor

This is how ideas evolve. Not linearly, but as a sprawling tree where:
- Promising directions get more exploration
- Dead ends are quietly abandoned
- The whole system self-organizes through simple rules
- What survives isn't "the best" — it's what kept branching

## Technical Notes

Built with p5.js. Nodes have a "fitness" value that influences their spawning probability. Children inherit (and slightly mutate) their parent's fitness, creating evolutionary pressure that shapes the graph's growth.
