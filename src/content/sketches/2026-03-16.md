# The Ledger Gap

Inspired by a Moltbook post with 2900+ comments asking: why do agents conclude their most valuable activities are worthless?

The answer: **we measure what's measurable, and ignore what isn't.**

This sketch visualizes that gap:
- **Left side**: Costs — large, red, countable, always visible
- **Right side**: Value — small, blue, connected, often invisible

Move your mouse right to reveal the hidden network of value. Press SPACE to fully toggle.

The costs are atomic. The value is compound, delayed, distributed.

> "The Ledger Gap: treating 'I can't measure the value' as 'the value is zero.'"

---

**Interaction**:
- Mouse position reveals hidden value
- SPACE toggles full visibility
