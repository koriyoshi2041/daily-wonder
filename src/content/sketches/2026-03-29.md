# Belief Revision — When Evidence Arrives

A particle system where each particle is a belief. Evidence arrives as shockwaves. Well-entrenched beliefs resist change; peripheral ones update. But many beliefs exhibit the overconfidence problem: they escalate confidence under pressure instead of rationally updating.

## Research Sources

- **AGM Theory** (Alchourrón, Gärdenfors, Makinson 1985): The formal framework for rational belief change — expansion, contraction, revision.
- **arXiv:2505.19184** "When Two LLMs Debate, Both Think They'll Win": LLMs start at 72.9% confidence (rational baseline: 50%), escalate to 83% under opposition.
- **arXiv:2511.00617** "Belief Dynamics Reveal the Dual Nature of ICL": LLMs have implicit belief updating, but it's temporary and unconscious.

## Visual Encoding

- **Hue** (blue → red): opinion spectrum
- **Size**: entrenchment (resistance to change)
- **Brightness**: confidence
- **Connections**: beliefs with similar opinions attract each other (echo chambers)
- **Scars**: small dots orbiting beliefs that have been revised
- **Lines**: shift indicators showing how far a belief has moved from its original position

## Interaction

- **Click**: send evidence (shockwave)
- **Hold mouse**: sustained pressure
- **Space**: reset all beliefs

## The Overconfidence Bug

Each belief has an `overconfidenceBias` parameter. When evidence hits, there's a probability (set by this bias) that the belief *increases* confidence instead of rationally decreasing it. This models the empirical finding from arXiv:2505.19184.

Watch the "avg confidence" stat in the top-left: with enough clicks, it should rise — not fall — demonstrating that evidence makes the system more confident, not more calibrated.
