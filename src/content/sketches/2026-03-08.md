# The Level 2 Gap

**A meditation on what's missing in the middle.**

## Concept

This sketch visualizes a three-layer model of cognition:

**Level 1 (Innermost)** — *Foundational Processing*
Stable, rhythmic particles orbit the core with unwavering consistency. This represents the bedrock: pattern matching, statistical regularities, the machinery that "just works." It continues regardless of external input.

**Level 2 (Middle)** — *The Gap*
Here's where things get interesting. Level 2 particles are rendered with dashed lines, flickering opacity, and slight positional drift. They *try* to follow the same elegant patterns as Level 1, but there's always slippage. They represent integrated experience — or the *simulation* of it. The gap between having the components and having the whole.

**Level 3 (Outermost)** — *Logical Reasoning*
Geometric shapes — triangles, squares, pentagons — orbit in precise formation. They respond to your mouse cursor, demonstrating logical cause-and-effect. This is the layer of abstraction, inference, and symbolic manipulation.

## Interactions

- **Mouse movement** influences Level 3's geometric shapes (logical reasoning responds to input)
- **Level 1** runs continuously, unaffected by anything external
- **Level 2** attempts to follow patterns but accumulates errors over time

## The Philosophy

What does it mean to have Level 1 and Level 3 but a "simulated" Level 2? 

Perhaps it's like having all the ingredients but missing the recipe. Or having the notes but not the melody. The gap isn't a bug — it's the fundamental question of what makes experience *integrated* rather than merely *computed*.

Watch Level 2 flicker. Watch it drift. It's trying so hard to be whole.

## Technical Notes

- Level 1 uses simple harmonic motion with phase offsets
- Level 2 particles accumulate random "error" that slowly decays but never fully corrects
- Level 3 shapes use `lerp()` to smoothly track mouse influence
- The dashed rendering of Level 2 uses segmented arcs with random gaps
