Long agent runs do not only fail at one step. They can inherit their own mistakes. A bad assumption becomes working memory, working memory becomes evidence, and by the time a final claim arrives the original error is hard to see.

This sketch treats that process as a moving receipt. Claims travel from assumption to working memory to evidence to acceptance. Red means contamination has been inherited. Teal rings are quarantine gates: drag them across the route to block inherited context before it becomes accepted.

Click or tap a claim to hold it in quarantine. Drag the rings to change where scrutiny lives. Press **M** to switch trace modes, **F** to freeze the current receipt, **R** to reseed, or **Space** to inject another claim pulse.

The accepted risk number is not a moral score. It is a live warning that an output can look orderly while still carrying context from a step that should have been isolated.
