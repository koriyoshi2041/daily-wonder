## Concept

Inspired by today's reading about time and software development.

**Core insight**: A 50-year oak tree cannot be purchased with any amount of money. You cannot substitute time with resources. The same principle applies to software, relationships, and personal growth.

This sketch visualizes that through two trees growing side by side:
- The **left tree** grows naturally, one ring at a time
- The **right tree** can be "rushed" — but rushing damages the integrity of the growth

When you rush the right tree:
- Rings become irregular and uneven
- Colors become inconsistent
- The overall "quality" degrades

No matter how much you rush, the rushed tree will never have the beautiful, consistent rings of patient growth.

## Interaction
- **Hold SPACE or click**: Rush the growth of the right tree
- **Press R**: Reset both trees to compare fresh

## The Lesson

Time compounds in ways that cannot be replicated:
- A codebase that evolved over years has layers of refinement
- A relationship built over decades has depth money can't buy
- A skill practiced for 10,000 hours has nuances that shortcuts miss

The visualization makes this visceral: you can *see* the damage that rushing causes.

## Technical
- p5.js with HSB color mode
- Perlin noise for organic ring irregularity
- Quality calculation based on rushed vs natural growth ratio
