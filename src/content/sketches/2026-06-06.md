Understanding behaves less like a stack of notes and more like cloth under tension. A claim can look complete from far away, but the weave only holds if observation, definition, counterexample, test, transfer, and memory keep pulling against each other.

This sketch turns that pressure into a loom. Drag a strand to bias the weave, tap the field to probe it, double tap an anchor to pin it, and use **1-3** to switch between weave, x-ray, and repel layers. Press **F** to freeze the current state, **R** to reseed the board, or **Space** to send another shuttle through the current strongest connection.

The comprehension score is deliberately unstable. It rises when the claim has both test pressure and counterexample pressure nearby, not when everything merely collapses into one neat cluster.
